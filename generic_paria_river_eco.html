Paria River Ecological Conservation

Overview:Since their introduction in the early 1900s, invasive tamarisk and Russian olive plants have dominated riparian areas throughout the American Southwest by rapidly spreading and competing with native vegetation for habitat and water resources. As of now, there have been limited efforts to monitor these invasive species throughout the Paria River watershed in southern Utah and northern Arizona. Using NASA remote sensing data and field survey data from our partners, we monitored the growing season phenology and aimed to model the spatial distribution of these invasive species throughout the watershed.

I presented this project at the 2025 Grand Staircase-Escalante Symposium. I also led a field trip into a tributary of the Paria River. We identified the target invasive species and disucssed the implications of GIS and modeling for management.

Project Contributors: Truman Anarella, Max VanArnan, Annie Kowalski, Wesley Rancher
